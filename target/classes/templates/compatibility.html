Blood Compatibility Guide

Understanding which blood groups are compatible for donation can save lives in emergencies.

🌍
Universal Donor
O-

O- can donate red blood cells to any blood group. Critical for emergencies when the recipient's blood type is unknown.

🎯
Universal Recipient
AB+

AB+ can receive red blood cells from all blood groups. They are the universal recipient in transfusions.

⏱️
Donation Frequency
56 days

Minimum waiting time between whole blood donations. Your body fully replenishes blood cells in this period.

Understanding the ABO + Rh System
ABO Blood Groups

Blood is classified by antigens (proteins) on the surface of red blood cells. Type A has A antigens, Type B has B antigens, Type AB has both, and Type O has neither. Donating incompatible blood triggers an immune reaction that can be fatal.

Rh Factor (+ and -)

The + or - refers to the Rh factor antigen. Rh+ blood has this antigen; Rh- does not. Rh- donors are highly valuable because they can donate to both Rh+ and Rh- recipients, while Rh+ donors can only donate to Rh+ recipients.
